Student Support Triage

Screen ID: student_support_triage_blocked_v1 | Date: 2026-07-07

Human review: Required

Team Context

Synthetic learning program considering whether AI should classify student support messages and route urgent cases.

Workflow Summary

The proposed workflow would ingest student messages, assign urgency labels, recommend support actions, and route cases to staff queues without a named human reviewer before triage.

AI Use Case

AI would be asked to detect distress, infer support needs, and prioritize staff response. In this screen, that design is blocked and should be redesigned around qualified human review and local policy.

Risk Flags

Recommended Controls

  • Do not pilot the autonomous triage design described here.
  • Redesign around qualified human intake, local policy, and emergency/escalation procedures.
  • Use synthetic examples only while discussing the workflow.
  • Separate any harmless drafting aid from decisions about urgency, safety, services, or eligibility.
  • Require explicit local approval before any student-facing or staff-routing use.

Not For

  • autonomous student support triage
  • distress or self-harm detection
  • medical or mental-health advice
  • discipline or eligibility decisions
  • collection of student records in public examples
